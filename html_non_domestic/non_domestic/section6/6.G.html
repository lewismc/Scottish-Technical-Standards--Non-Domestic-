6.G.0 Introduction
Scottish Ministers have powers under section 25 of the Building (Scotland)
Act 2003 to make a Direction to local authorities where they consider that
buildings of any description to which building regulations apply ought to
comply with a provision of the regulations.
This power is now exercised to direct local authorities to secure improvement
to the energy performance of existing non- domestic buildings. Directions to
local authorities, which enable the following measures, are published on the
Building Standards Division website.

6.G.1 Application
The Directions apply to new work to an existing building, where such work
includes the provision of new fixed building services or extends the capacity
of existing fixed building services and works which:
a. are subject to an application for building warrant; and
b. are subject to standards 6.3, 6.4, 6.5 or 6.6.
In such cases, existing fixed building services of the same general type as
those within the proposed new work should be improved to meet the current
performance recommendations given in guidance to standards 6.3 to 6.6.
Where proposals include work subject to one of the standards listed below,
improvement to the corresponding elements of the existing installation should
be made. Where new works include work subject to:
•
standard 6.3 (heating systems), upgrading of heating controls and, where
more than 15 years old, replacement of existing boiler;
•
standard 6.4 (insulation of pipes, ducts and vessels), upgrading of
insulation to hot water storage vessels;
•
standard 6.5 (artificial and display lighting), upgrading of lighting controls
and luminaires to current levels of efficiency/efficacy; and
•
standard 6.6 (ventilation and cooling), upgrading of ventilation controls
and, if more than 10 years old, replacement of existing air-conditioning
chiller units.
6.G.2 Scale of improvement
It is intended that improvement works should not be disproportionate in scale
to the proposed new works . Accordingly, where proposed new works are
limited in scale, improvement required to the existing building should be
representative of this.
Improvements identified are considered to offer a payback period of around
five years or, in the case of boiler and chiller unit replacement, prompt
replacement of equipment that is nearing the end of its useful life.
6.G.3 Exceptions
In respect of all of the above, improvement need not be made where current
installations were both subject to requirements of a relevant functional or
technical standards applicable from 4 th March 2002 and already meet or
exceed such performance standards. These standards are available on the
Building Standards Division Technical Handbooks webpage.
6.G.4 Further guidance
Supplementary guidance on the assessment and implementation of
improvements to existing building services, including examples, will
be made available on the BSD Technical Guidance web page.
